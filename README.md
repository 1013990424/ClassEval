# Building a Multimodal Classroom Evaluation Benchmark via Dynamic and Explainable Automated Annotation

This is the official PyTorch code for the paper "[[Building a Multimodal Classroom Evaluation Benchmark via Dynamic and Explainable Automated Annotation]]()".

#### 

> **Abstract:** Multimodal large language models (MLLMs) have shown strong capabilities in video understanding and cross-modal reasoning, yet their application to educational scenarios remains underexplored. In particular, the lack of dedicated classroom benchmarks and reliable evaluation protocols for long-form report generation limits progress in this domain. To address this gap, we present \textbf{ClassEval}, a large-scale multimodal classroom benchmark consisting of 1,065 real-world classroom videos paired with human-refined evaluation reports. To enable scalable and reliable dataset construction, we propose a semi-automatic annotation pipeline that integrates multimodal reasoning with human verification. Specifically, we introduce an OCR-enhanced transcript refinement mechanism to correct domain-specific ASR errors, and a segment-aware keyframe selection strategy to reduce visual redundancy while preserving pedagogically meaningful events. Based on structured multimodal evidence, our pipeline generates draft reports that are further refined by human annotators to ensure factual correctness and consistency. In addition, we establish a comprehensive evaluation protocol for classroom report generation, including both structured automatic metrics and human assessment, enabling systematic comparison across models. Extensive experiments on ClassEval demonstrate the effectiveness of our benchmark and reveal the limitations of existing models in fine-grained educational understanding. We hope our work facilitates future research on multimodal reasoning in authentic classroom scenarios.
